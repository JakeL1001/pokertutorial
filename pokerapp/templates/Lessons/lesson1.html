Welcome to our first poker lesson. A deck of playing cards consists of four groups called suits. These suits are spades and clubs which are commonly coloured black. As well as hearts and diamonds, commonly red.


Each suit has thirteen cards. The cards have a numerical order. There are the "face" cards of ace, king, queen and jack. The ace can act as a number one but usally counts as the highest value in the suit. Following it, the king acts as the thirteenth card, the queen twelth and jack eleventh.


A suit also has the cards two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine and ten. These rank in their natural order of 2 through 10. If you had the tenth card in the suit of clubs. It could be referred to as a "ten of clubs".

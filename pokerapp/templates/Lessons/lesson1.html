Welcome to our first poker lesson. A deck of playing cards consists of four groups called suits. These suits are spades and clubs which are commonly coloured black. As well as hearts and diamonds, commonly red.

Each suit has thirteen cards. The cards have a numerical order. There are the "face" cards of ace, jack, queen and king. The ace acts as the number one. As well as the highest value in the suit. The jack acts as the eleventh card, the queen twelth and king thirteenth. A suit also has the cards two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine and ten. If you had the tenth card in the suit of clubs. It could be referred to as a "ten of clubs."

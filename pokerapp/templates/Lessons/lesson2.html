Hand Rankings

In this lesson you will learn about the different poker "hands". A hand is a combination of five cards. Players have two cards in their hand and at the end of of each round there are five cards on the table. Each player makes their hand by choosing what cards from the table or their hand give them the highest ranking. The highest ranked hand out of all players wins. If multiple players have hands with the same 'rank', the highest number card within this rank wins (recall lesson 1 on card rankings).



 High Card 



The lowest ranked hand in poker is 'high card'. This happens when none of the other hands can be made and the player is left with just their highest card.


 Pair 



The next ranked hand if 'pair' or 'two of a kind'. This happens when a player has two matching cards.

  Two Pair 



The next ranked hand is 'two pair'. This is where a player has two sets of matching cards.

 Three of a Kind 



The next ranked hand is 'thre of a kind' or 'trips'. This is where a player has 3 of one type of card.

 Straight 



A straight is when a player has 5 cards in consecutive rank order. The order on the board does not matter, it can be rearranged to the players desire. Recall from the last lesson that an ace can be a 1 or a 14.

 Flush 



A flush is when a player has 5 cards all of the same suit.

 Full House 



A Full House is when a player has 3 of the same cards as well as 2 of a different type. The same as having three of a kind and a pair.

 Four of a Kind 



Four of a kind or quads is when a player has all 4 of the same type of card. Their are only 4 of each type of card, one for each suit, in the whole card deck!

 Straight Flush 



Like the name suggests a 'Straight Flush' is when a player both has a straight and a flush at the same time. The player must have 5 cards in order and of the same suit. This is the strongest hand in poker, and when it starts from the highest card (the ace) it is known as the Royal Flush.

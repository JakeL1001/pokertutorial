Welcome to our second poker lesson. In this lesson you will learn about the different poker "hands". In the game of poker, the player with the best hand wins. A hand is a combination of five cards. Players have two cards in their hand and at the end of of each round there are five cards on the table. Here is a chart of the poker hands that are possible.

Players compete to have the best five out of seven cards at the end of a round (unless all other players have 'folded'). The first way to win a round of poker is called having a high card. This means one of your cards are higher in the suit than the other players. An example is if you have 'ace high' and the other player has a 'ten high'. Then you win that round. This concept applies to all the other possible hands. If one player has a pair, and the other has a two pair. The player with a two pair will win that round. The hands are ranked in the order shown above and the goal is to have a better hand than the other players. If players have the same effective hand, they will 'split the pot.'
